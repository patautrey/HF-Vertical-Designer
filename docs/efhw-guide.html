End-Fed Half-Wave (EFHW) Vertical Guide

This guide explains the behavior, construction, and performance of EFHW vertical antennas. EFHWs are among the most efficient portable antennas available, especially for DX work on 20m, 15m, and 10m. They use a high-ratio transformer (49:1 or 64:1) to match the extremely high feedpoint impedance of a half-wave wire.

1. EFHW Fundamentals

An End-Fed Half-Wave antenna is exactly what it sounds like: a wire cut to one half-wavelength on the primary band, fed at the end.

1.1 High Feedpoint Impedance

A half-wave wire fed at the end exhibits a very high impedance:

  • Typically 2,000–5,000 Ω
  • Requires a 49:1 or 64:1 transformer
  • Without the transformer, SWR is extreme

1.2 Harmonic Resonance

EFHWs are naturally resonant on harmonics:

  • 20m EFHW → 20m, 10m, 40m (with tuner)
  • 15m EFHW → 15m, 10m, 6m
  • 40m EFHW → 40m, 20m, 15m, 10m
Key Advantage: EFHWs provide multi-band coverage with minimal complexity.

2. Required Counterpoise

EFHW verticals MUST have a short counterpoise attached at the transformer. Without it:

  • RF flows down the coax shield
  • Pattern becomes distorted
  • Feedline radiates (RF in shack)
  • Tuner may behave unpredictably

2.1 Recommended Length

Counterpoise length ≈ 5% of wavelength:

  • 20m EFHW → ~1 m counterpoise
  • 40m EFHW → ~2 m counterpoise
Rule: Always attach the counterpoise directly to the transformer chassis.

3. EFHW Transformer Ratios

3.1 49:1 UNUN

Standard for most EFHWs. Works well for:

  • 20m EFHW (33 ft)
  • 40m EFHW (65 ft)

3.2 64:1 UNUN

Best for 15m EFHWs. Provides:

  • Lower SWR
  • Less reflected power
  • Reduced transformer heating

3.3 56:1 (Theoretical)

Mathematical modeling suggests 56:1 is ideal for 15m, but these transformers are rare. 64:1 is the practical choice.

4. EFHW Vertical Profiles

4.1 20m EFHW Vertical (33 ft)

The premier DX vertical. Outstanding low-angle radiation. Works 20m, 10m, and 40m (with tuner).

Wire Length33 ft / 10 m
Transformer49:1
Counterpoise1 m
Primary Band20m
Harmonics10m, 40m
Field Note: This antenna consistently outperforms quarter-wave verticals on 20m.

4.2 15m EFHW Vertical (21 ft 11 in)

Extremely efficient on 21 MHz. Transformer ratio is critical.

Wire Length21 ft 11 in / 6.69 m
Transformer64:1 (best), 49:1 (acceptable)
SWR1.3–1.4 with 64:1
Harmonics10m, 6m

4.3 40m EFHW Vertical (65 ft)

Full-size 40m EFHW. Works 40m, 20m, 15m, 10m. Requires tall support or sloper configuration.

5. Helical EFHW Verticals

When the support mast is shorter than the wire, the upper portion can be wrapped in a loose helix.

5.1 Why Helical Works

Helical winding:

  • Preserves electrical length
  • Reduces physical height
  • Changes feedpoint impedance

5.2 Transformer Impact

A helical 33 ft wire behaves differently than a straight one:

  • 4:1 UNUN → too low ratio → high SWR
  • 9:1 UNUN → matches well across 40–10m
Rule: After any helical modification, re-evaluate transformer ratio.
