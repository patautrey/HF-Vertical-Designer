Rybakov & Random-Wire Vertical Guide

This guide explains the “magic lengths” used in the Rybakov family of portable HF vertical antennas. These lengths are intentionally chosen to avoid resonance on amateur bands, keeping feedpoint impedance within a manageable range for tuners and UNUNs.

All data and behavior in this guide is based on extensive field testing, including the work of Walt (K4OGO) and the Coastal Waves & Wires methodology.

1. Why These Lengths Work

Random wire lengths often land near resonance on one or more bands, causing:

  • Very high or very low impedance
  • Stress on transformer cores
  • ATU overload or failure to tune
  • Unpredictable SWR behavior

The Rybakov lengths avoid these resonance points. This keeps impedance moderate and tunable across many bands.

Key Principle: Non-resonant lengths + correct UNUN ratio = stable multi-band performance.

2. The Rybakov Family

These are the five proven lengths used in portable HF vertical operations.

Antenna Length UNUN Best Bands Notes
17-Five 17.5 ft 4:1 20m–6m Excellent near saltwater on 40m
Coastal 20 20.5 ft 4:1 10m, 11m, 20m 5/8-wave on 10m → low-angle DX
Rybakov 25 25 ft 4:1 40m–6m Classic all-band performer
Random 29 29 ft 9:1 40m–10m 1:1 SWR on 10m naturally
Long 41 41 ft 9:1 80m–10m Requires helical wrap on 10m pole

3. Individual Antenna Profiles

3.1 17-Five (17.5 ft)

The smallest Rybakov. Extremely portable. Works 20m–6m easily with a 4:1 UNUN. Near saltwater, it unexpectedly performs well on 40m.

3.2 Coastal 20 (20.5 ft)

Exactly 5/8-wave on 10 meters. Produces natural low-angle radiation ideal for DX. Excellent on 20m as well.

3.3 Rybakov 25 (25 ft)

The gold standard. Avoids resonance on all HF bands. Tunes 40m–6m with a 4:1 UNUN and 4–7 radials.

3.4 Random 29 (29 ft)

The most popular random-wire vertical. With a 9:1 UNUN, it covers 40m–10m. On 10m, it presents a natural 1:1 SWR.

3.5 Long 41 (41 ft)

Reaches 80m with a tuner. Requires helical winding on a 10 m pole. Excellent for overnight or multi-day portable ops.

4. Transformer Selection

Correct transformer ratio is essential:

  • 4:1 → 17.5 ft, 20.5 ft, 25 ft
  • 9:1 → 29 ft, 41 ft
Warning: Using a 49:1 on a random wire will cause extreme SWR and core heating.

5. Radials & Grounding

Rybakov and random-wire verticals require radials for efficiency.

  • 4–7 radials at ~16 ft each
  • More radials = diminishing returns after 8–10
  • Saltwater dramatically improves performance
Saltwater Technique:
Lay radials in wet sand or dip wire tips into the water for maximum low-angle gain.
